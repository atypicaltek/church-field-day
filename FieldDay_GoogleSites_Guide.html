⛪ Church Field Day — Google Sites Setup Guide

Step-by-step instructions to create your volunteer signup website and embed all 11 Google Forms. No coding required.

⏱ ~45 minutes total 🖥 Desktop browser recommended 📧 Requires Google account 💲 100% free

1

Run the Form Builder Script (creates all 11 forms automatically)

⏱ ~5 min
This step uses FieldDay_CreateForms.gs — a Google Apps Script that auto-creates every form and links them all to one master spreadsheet. You run it once and it does the rest.
1

Open Google Apps Script

In a new browser tab, go to script.google.com and sign in with your church Google account.

2

Create a new project

Click New project (top left). The editor opens with a blank Code.gs file.

3

Paste the script

Open FieldDay_CreateForms.gs in a text editor, select all, copy, then paste it into the Apps Script editor — replacing the existing code completely.

4

Run the function

In the dropdown that says "Select function," choose createAllFieldDayForms. Then click the ▶ Run button.

⚠️ A permissions popup will appear. Click Review permissions → Advanced → Go to project (unsafe) → Allow. This is safe — it's your own script accessing your own Google account.
5

Collect your form URLs

When done (≈ 2–3 min), click View → Logs. You'll see all 11 form URLs. Also open Google Drive — a folder called "Field Day Forms" holds all forms, and a spreadsheet called "Field Day Signups — Master" has a Form Links & Embed Codes tab with every URL in one place.

✅ The "Embed URL" column is what you'll paste into Google Sites in Phase 5.
2

Create Your Google Site

⏱ ~5 min
1

Go to Google Sites

In a new tab, go to sites.google.com. Click the large ＋ (Blank site) button.

2

Name your site

At the top left, click "Enter site name" and type: Church Field Day Signups (or your preferred name). Press Enter.

3

Pick a theme

In the right panel, click Themes. Choose any theme — Simple or Diplomat work well for a clean, professional look. You can change colors to match your church brand.

4

Edit the home page banner

Click the top banner area. Change the title text to: ⛪ Church Field Day — Volunteer Signup. Add a subtitle: 400–800 Attendees · 8 Sites · 11 Activities. Optionally upload your church logo as the background image.

💡 Drag pages in the Pages panel to reorder them — your navigation menu will match.
4

Embed the Live Dashboard on the Home Page

⏱ ~3 min
The FieldDay_Live_Dashboard.html file needs to be hosted somewhere public before it can be embedded. The easiest free option is GitHub Pages (free, takes 2 minutes).
1

Host the dashboard on GitHub Pages (free)

  • Go to github.com → Sign in or create a free account
  • Click New repository → name it fielddaydashboard → set to Public → Create
  • Click Add file → Upload files → drag in FieldDay_Live_Dashboard.html
  • Rename it to index.html in the upload dialog → Commit
  • Go to Settings → Pages → Source: main branch → Save
  • Your dashboard URL will be: https://[your-username].github.io/fielddaydashboard/
✅ GitHub Pages is completely free for public sites. No account required beyond a basic GitHub login.
2

Embed it in Google Sites

On the Home page in Google Sites: click Insert → Embed → paste your GitHub Pages URL → click Insert. Resize the embed block to fill most of the page.

3

Add a text section above the embed

Insert a Text box with a welcome message explaining the event, deadline reminders, and which site each visitor is from.

5

Embed All 11 Google Forms (one per activity page)

⏱ ~12 min (~1 min each)
✅ Google Sites embeds Google Forms natively — no hosting needed. This is the easiest part!
1

Open the "Form Links & Embed Codes" tab

Go to your master spreadsheet → open the Form Links & Embed Codes tab. Keep this open in a separate browser tab for reference.

2

For each activity page, embed its form

In Google Sites, navigate to the activity page (e.g., Food Signup). Then:

  • Click Insert → Google Forms (it's in the right panel under "From Drive")
  • Find the matching form (e.g., "Field Day — Food Signup") → click it
  • The form embeds automatically, full width

3

Add a description above each form

Insert a Text box above each embedded form explaining what volunteers in that role do, the deadline, and how many are needed from their site. Example for Food page:

Suggested text for Food Signup page 🍽 Food Servers Signup — Deadline: 3 weeks before event

Each site is pre-assigned a dish by the Food Chair. Sign up here to be a server at your site's food station. Each station needs enough volunteers to serve 400–800 attendees:
  • The Cathedral: 12 servers needed
  • All other sites: 8 servers needed each
4

Repeat for all 11 activity pages

Each page gets one embedded Google Form. Use the table below as your checklist:

  • Drinks Signup page → "Field Day — Drinks Signup" form
  • Games Signup page → "Field Day — Games Signup" form
  • DJ / Music page → "Field Day — DJ / Music Requests" form
  • Karaoke Signup page → "Field Day — Karaoke Signup" form
  • Basketball page → "Field Day — Basketball Tournament Registration" form
  • Kid Games page → "Field Day — Kid Games Signup" form
  • Parking page → "Field Day — Parking Volunteer Signup" form

  • Pretzels page → "Field Day — Pretzels Signup" form
6

Add Navigation, Branding & Announcements

⏱ ~5 min
1

Navigation menu

Google Sites auto-generates a top navigation bar from your pages. To add a dropdown grouping (e.g., "Signups" → 11 sub-pages): in the Pages panel, drag child pages underneath a parent page. A dropdown group will appear automatically in the nav.

💡 Suggested nav structure: Home · Signups ▾ (11 sub-pages) · Volunteer Info
2

Header logo & colors

Click the site header area → Change image to upload your church logo or a field day graphic. Go to Themes → Customize to match your church brand colors.

3

Volunteer Info page

On the Volunteer Info page, insert a Table or paste the requirements matrix directly (copy from the Excel file). Add deadline reminders and site lead contact info here.

4

Add a site-wide announcement banner

On every page, add a text section at the top with the event date and a short reminder. Example: "🗓 Event Date: [Date TBD] · Signups close 2–3 weeks before · Questions? Contact your Site Lead."

7

Publish & Share the Site

⏱ ~2 min
1

Publish the site

Click the blue Publish button (top right). Your URL will be: sites.google.com/view/church-field-day-signups (or your chosen name — you can customize it here).

⚠️ Choose "Anyone with the link" or "Anyone" under who can view — otherwise volunteers outside your Google org won't be able to access the forms.
2

Share with Site Leads

Copy the published URL and send it to all 8 Site Leads via text/email/group chat. They share it with their members. Each volunteer clicks the link, navigates to their activity page, and fills out the form — responses go directly into the master spreadsheet.

3

Optional: custom domain

If your church has a website, you can link it. Go to Settings → Custom domain and follow Google's instructions to map a subdomain like fielddayfbc.com.

8

Print QR Codes for Each Form Page

⏱ ~5 min
1

Get your page URLs

After publishing, each page has its own URL. Navigate to each page on the live site and copy the URL from your browser bar. They'll look like:
sites.google.com/view/church-field-day-signups/food-signup

2

Generate QR codes (free, no account needed)

Go to qr-code-generator.com. Paste each page URL → download the QR code as a PNG. Alternatively, in Chrome: click the address bar → Share → Create QR code → download.

3

Print and post at the event

Print each QR code on a half-sheet or full sheet with the activity name and station description. Post at the central signup table and at each activity station. The FieldDay_Signup_Portal.html file (from your earlier files) also has a printable QR grid page for all 11 codes at once.

💡 Use the QR portal page's "Home / QR Codes" tab to print all 11 QR codes at once — just update the form URLs to your live Google Sites page URLs inside the script for the portal.
